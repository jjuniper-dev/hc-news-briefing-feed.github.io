Assessment

This page is the starting point for a fit-checking tool and a procurement issues flagger focused on product and service reviews. It is designed to surface nuanced sovereignty, control, and operational risk.

What this system evaluates

  • Strategic fit
  • Data sovereignty
  • Infrastructure control
  • Funding dependency
  • Procurement red flags

Case example: Cohere North

The example below demonstrates exactly the type of mixed-signal profile this assessment system should flag.

Signals in favor

✓ Domestic strategic alignment

  • Cohere is Canadian-founded and headquartered in Toronto.
  • SAP announced (February 10, 2026) integration of Cohere North into SAP’s Canadian Sovereign Cloud.
  • The federal government committed $240M to Cohere’s $725M Cambridge, Ontario data centre effort.

Flags requiring review

⚠ Sovereignty and control ambiguity

  • The Cambridge facility is operated by CoreWeave, a U.S. provider.
  • Cohere has accepted U.S. venture capital, introducing potential foreign influence vectors.
  • Control, access, and continuity terms must be validated in procurement documents.

Initial scoring model (v0)

Each assessment can generate a concise profile such as: Fit: High | Sovereignty: Mixed | Procurement Risk: Medium-High

  • Fit score: Alignment to use-case, mission, and capability maturity.
  • Sovereignty score: Jurisdiction, operational control, and data residency reality.
  • Procurement risk score: Contractual lock-in, third-party dependencies, and governance gaps.
